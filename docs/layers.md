# The Layers: What's Acceptable, and What Crosses the Line

Each layer's actors defend themselves with *"that's normal."* This file removes the excuse
by writing the standard down. The test is not "is this one act a big deal" — it's
**"would this be acceptable if the person on the receiving end could see it stacked with
everything else?"**

For each layer: what's **normal**, what's a **gray zone** (track it), and what's a **line
crossed** (act on it).

---

## Layer 1 — Workplace / Professional

Harm here looks like blocked progress in a disclosed field: opportunities routed around you,
credit withheld, a quiet freeze-out.

| Normal | Gray zone — log it | Line crossed — act |
|---|---|---|
| Disagreement, honest feedback, losing a fair competition | Repeated exclusion from meetings you belong in; credit reassigned; "coincidental" stalls | Retaliation after a complaint; harassment tied to a protected class; sabotage; threats |
| One person not liking you | A pattern across people that tracks one event (you spoke up, you disclosed something) | Anything that would violate your employer's own written policy or EEOC standards |

**Why the local-knowledge problem bites hardest here:** each manager, each peer, sees one
decision and calls it merit. The aggregate — that every door closes the same way — is
invisible to all of them and obvious only to you. Log the doors.

---

## Layer 2 — Social / Online

| Normal | Gray zone — log it | Line crossed — act |
|---|---|---|
| Not being included in everything; people drifting | Coordinated exclusion; rumors you can trace; a pile-on that follows you across spaces | Threats, doxxing, sustained targeted harassment, impersonation |

**Local-knowledge trap:** each person who repeats a rumor thinks they're just talking.
Nobody sees that they're the 30th node in a graph. Screenshot with timestamps and URLs.

---

## Layer 3 — Neighborhood / Public Space

| Normal | Gray zone — log it | Line crossed — act |
|---|---|---|
| Strangers being neutral or cold; ordinary friction | Repeated, recognizable provocations; "jokes" that recur; being followed once | A pattern of following; intimidation; property interference; anything that makes you reroute your life |

**Local-knowledge trap:** the person who stares or "jokes" experiences it as a single,
forgettable moment. You experience it as the tenth one this week. The date-stamped log is
what converts *their* forgettable moment into *your* documented pattern.

---

## Layer 4 — Home / Physical Security

This layer is different: here, "acceptable" has almost no gray zone. Entry, tampering, and
physical threat are not social friction — they are crimes.

| Normal | Line crossed — act immediately |
|---|---|
| Normal building/maintenance access you consented to and were notified of | Any unauthorized entry; signs of tampering; anything moved or taken; any physical threat |

**If you believe your home is being entered:**
1. **Document before touching anything** — photos, time, what's different. (See the log template.)
2. **File a police report each time.** Each report is a dated record; the pattern lives in the stack of reports, not any one.
3. **Make entry *evidenced*, not just *felt*:** entry sensors, a camera covering the door,
   a cheap tell-tale (a hair/tape across the frame, a witnessed seal). The goal is to turn
   "I think" into "here is the footage / here is the broken seal at this timestamp."
4. If reports pile up without response, that itself becomes documentation for the next layer
   up (oversight, a lawyer, local press). See [`escalation.md`](escalation.md).

Evidence is everything here. A claim of daily entry with no artifacts is the hardest thing
in the world to act on; the same claim with thirty timestamped clips is the easiest.

---

## Layer 5 — Institutional

The layer that's *supposed* to catch the others: HR, police, courts, agencies, oversight.

| Working as intended | Line crossed — escalate past it |
|---|---|
| A complaint logged, investigated, answered | Complaints repeatedly "lost"; no response; the body that should protect you mirrors the harm |

When an institution fails to act, **the failure is itself loggable** and is often what
unlocks the next level (an agency over the institution, a lawyer, a journalist). Document the
non-response with the same discipline as the original incident.

---

## The one rule that ties every layer together

> **No single act is the case. The dated aggregate is the case.**

Every layer's defense is isolation. Your entire job is de-isolation: one log, every layer,
one timeline.
