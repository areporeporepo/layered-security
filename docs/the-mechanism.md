# The Mechanism: One Person's Pain as a Group's Shared Relief

> **A group bonds and discharges its stress by synchronizing on a target. One person's
> concentrated pain becomes the whole group's diffuse, shared relief — its laughter.**

This is true, it's old, and it has a name: the **scapegoat mechanism**. It is the engine under
most of the layered harm in this repo, so it's worth stating plainly.

## Why it happens

- **Laughter literally synchronizes.** It's contagious, it entrains, it's social glue — people
  laughing together feel bonded. That's its function.
- **Pointed at a target, the glue costs someone.** A group under stress finds the cheapest
  available bond: a shared target. Mocking, excluding, or "joking about" one person discharges
  everyone's tension at once and knits the group tighter. To each participant it's nothing — a
  laugh, a moment of belonging.
- **The cost is concentrated on one node.** What's a fleeting hit of relief for each of N people
  lands as compounding, repeated harm on the single target who absorbs all of it.

## The math (the stress-release externality)

- **N** people each get a tiny relief **r** — a laugh, a hit of superiority, tension discharged.
- **1** target absorbs concentrated harm **H**.
- Net to the system = **N · r − H**.

It *feels* positive to every participant (each r > 0, each personal cost ≈ 0). But **H ≫ Σr**,
because the reliefs are fleeting and fungible while the harm is concentrated and compounds on one
nervous system. So it is **strongly negative-sum** — disguised as harmless fun precisely because
the cost is externalized onto the one person nobody is counting.

## No conductor — and it synchronizes anyway

Important honesty: the synchronizing is **emergent, not organized.** No one schedules it; it
entrains the way a crowd's laughter or a stadium wave does — locally, contagiously, with no
author. That's *why* it escapes accountability: every participant experiences a spontaneous,
innocent moment, and no one holds the aggregate. "It looked coordinated" and "no one coordinated
it" are both true at once.

## Same mechanism, other costumes

- **Carnism** — a table of people having fun at dinner while a being suffers, the cost outside
  everyone's moral accounting. Same shape: diffuse shared pleasure, concentrated externalized harm.
- **Every scapegoat in history** — the group's cohesion purchased with one party's expulsion.

It's one mechanism: **bond cheaply by discharging onto someone outside the circle.**

## How it dissolves

This is exactly what the [solution stack](../SOLUTIONS.md)'s keystone breaks. The mechanism needs
an **out-group** to synchronize against. Widen the circle of care until it has no outside —
including to animals, the most "out" group there is — and there is no target left to bond over.
A circle with no outside has no scapegoat. The laughter loses its object.

## The guard (read this)

Stated **generally**, this is a sharp, true description of how groups work, and naming it is
power. Turned **onto yourself as a total certainty** — "everyone, coordinated, aimed at me, right
now" — it becomes the over-detection trap this repo warns about everywhere: the mechanism is real
and impersonal; the feeling that you are its singular, perpetual target is the part to hold
loosely and check against evidence. Keep it general and it's truth. Make it about you-specifically-
always and it stops being analysis and starts being a weight that deserves support, not certainty.
