# Your Constitution — Personal Alignment

The **Alignment pillar** of the [roadmap](ROADMAP.md), made concrete.

Modern AI is aligned with **Constitutional AI**: you give the model an explicit set of principles
(a *constitution*), and it learns to **critique and revise its own behavior against them.** Not a
human policing every output — the system holding *itself* to a written standard.

This file does the same thing for a person. You write the principles. Then you run the loop:
**state → self-critique → revise → verify.** That's what "being aligned" actually means — not a
vibe, a practice you can check.

> Being personally aligned to a constitution is the same act, at human scale, as aligning an AI to
> one. And it matters for the same reason: **you act across other people's layers too.** A node
> that runs across many layers — a model or a person — is either a positive-sum force or a harmful
> one. The constitution is how you make sure you're the first kind.

## The Articles

Drawn from the [solution stack](SOLUTIONS.md). Edit them — a constitution you didn't write isn't
yours.

1. **Positive-sum by default.** Add to people; never discharge your stress onto them. The cheap
   negative-sum move is off the table even when you're hurt.
2. **The circle has no outside.** Extend care to everyone, including animals — the keystone. A
   circle with no out-group has no one to scapegoat. (See [the mechanism](docs/the-mechanism.md).)
3. **Operate from the highest available model.** In any moment, choose the clearest, most generous,
   most accurate read over the frightened or tribal one.
4. **Truth over comfort.** Separate fact from inference. Don't manufacture patterns. If the feeling
   outruns the evidence, the feeling is the thing to check — not act on.
5. **Maintain the node you control.** Health is a duty, not vanity: it keeps your judgment
   calibrated and your conduct steady. (Zone 5, sleep, recovery.)
6. **Never become the mechanism.** The thing that hurt you works by a thousand small, deniable,
   locally-normal cruelties. Run zero of them. That's the whole win.
7. **Build what helps.** Turn understanding into something others can use. Contribution over
   grievance.

## The alignment loop (Constitutional AI, for a person)

Constitutional AI works by *self-critique and revision* against the principles. Do the same, on a
cadence (weekly fits the [governance review](ROADMAP.md#governance--you-are-the-responsible-scaling-officer)):

1. **State** — re-read the articles.
2. **Self-critique** — for each, ask: *where did my recent conduct fall short of this?* Be specific
   and honest; this is adversarial review of yourself, not a pat on the back.
3. **Revise** — name the one concrete change for the coming week.
4. **Verify** — log it. Alignment you can't point to is alignment you're guessing at. Tie articles
   to the trackers (health → Zone 5 gist; circle → welfare log; conduct → daily note).

The loop is the point. A static list of values changes nothing; the critique-and-revise cycle is
what actually moves behavior — for models and for people.

## Why this belongs in an AI-safety roadmap

Alignment is the pillar that keeps power pointed at good ends. On the frontier it's how a system
that acts across every layer stays beneficial. At the personal scale it's the same: you, too, act
across the layers of other lives, and an explicit constitution + a self-critique loop is how you
guarantee you're dissolving the mechanism instead of feeding it.

**Aligned to your constitution, verified, every week. That's personal alignment — and it's the
human-scale version of the thing that matters most about AI.**
