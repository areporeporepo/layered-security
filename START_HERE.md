# If this is happening to you — start here

You don't have to read the whole repo. You might not have the energy right now. This page is
the part that helps. Take it one line at a time.

---

## First: if you're in crisis right now

- **In immediate danger:** call **911** (US) or your local emergency number.
- **Being hurt by someone you live with or near:** **National Domestic Violence Hotline —
  call 1-800-799-7233, or text START to 88788.** 24/7, free, confidential. You don't have to
  be sure it "counts" to call.
- **The weight itself is crushing you / thoughts of not wanting to be here:** call or text
  **988** (US Suicide & Crisis Lifeline). It's for anyone carrying more than they can hold.

Outside the US, search "<your country> crisis line" — most have one.

**Do this part before the rest.** Documentation can wait an hour. Your safety can't.

---

## You are not crazy, and you are not the only one

What's happening to you is a real pattern: harm spread thin across many people and many parts
of your life, where each person's piece looks small and "normal" — so no one feels responsible,
and you're left holding the whole weight nobody else can see. It is not your failure. It is not
imagined. And it is not personal to you — it's a mechanism a lot of people are under. Naming it
is the first thing that helps. (The model is in the [README](README.md), for later.)

## Five concrete steps

### 1. Start a log today — even one line
Open a notes app or copy [`incidents.csv`](incidents.csv). For each thing that happens, write:
**date, time, what happened (just the facts, like a camera would see it), and a 1–5 severity.**
A feeling can't be acted on. A dated log can. This is the single highest-leverage thing you can do.

### 2. Separate what happened from what you think it means
Two columns: *facts* and *your read.* The facts are what HR, a lawyer, or police can use. Keeping
them apart also keeps you steady — over weeks you can see whether the pattern holds. Both matter.

### 3. Find the layer with the highest stakes right now
Your safety comes before your job. If anything physical is happening, that's the layer to act on
first — see [`docs/escalation.md`](docs/escalation.md). Don't try to fix every layer at once.

### 4. Take it to a channel with real power
A report by itself usually changes nothing — it's a *timestamp*, not an action. The things with
teeth: a **protective order**, a **lawyer** (many give free consults), **footage** from a camera,
or an **agency with jurisdiction**. Hand them the log. See [`docs/escalation.md`](docs/escalation.md).

### 5. Protect the one thing you control: you
You can't reprogram the people doing this. You *can* keep yourself clear enough to act.
Right now, today: one slow breath. A walk. Water. Sleep when you can. One real human you can
talk to. This isn't a consolation prize — a steady, clear, well-documented person handles this
far better than an exhausted one. See [`docs/baseline.md`](docs/baseline.md).

---

## The one rule to remember

> **No single act is the case. The dated total is the case. And you don't have to carry the
> total alone — that's what the log, the lawyer, and the people on the phone lines are for.**

Start with step 1, or with a phone call. Just start with one.
